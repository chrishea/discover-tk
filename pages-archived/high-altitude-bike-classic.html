Pedal Through the Pines

Every August, cyclists from across the Southwest converge on Cloudcroft for the High Altitude Bike Classic, one of New Mexico's premier cycling events. With routes winding through Lincoln National Forest and along the crest of the Sacramento Mountains, riders tackle challenging climbs, breathtaking descents, and thin mountain air at over 9,000 feet. Whether you're a seasoned road cyclist chasing a personal best or a mountain biker craving singletrack through the pines, this ride delivers an unforgettable high-altitude experience.

The event draws riders of all levels with multiple route options ranging from family-friendly loops to grueling century rides with thousands of feet of climbing. Post-ride festivities in the village make for a perfect summer weekend in the mountains.

Event Highlights

More Than Just a Ride

The High Altitude Bike Classic is a full weekend of cycling culture in the mountains.

🏔️

High Altitude Challenge

Ride at 9,000 feet where thin air tests your fitness and cool mountain temperatures make for perfect cycling weather.

🌲

Lincoln National Forest

Routes wind through towering Ponderosa pines, past mountain meadows, and along ridgelines with views stretching to White Sands.

🍻

Post-Ride Festival

Celebrate your finish at the village with local craft beer from Cloudcroft Brewery, live music, and food from local restaurants.

🏆

Category awards, raffle prizes, and finisher swag. Every rider gets a commemorative jersey and medal.

Rider Information

Plan Your Ride Weekend

Everything you need to know to make the most of your High Altitude Bike Classic experience.

📋

Registration

Online registration opens in May. Early bird pricing available through June. Day-of registration subject to availability.

🅿️

Packet Pickup

Packet pickup Saturday afternoon at the Lodge Pavilion. Includes bib number, timing chip, route map, and rider swag bag.

🏨

Where to Stay

Book early! Cloudcroft lodging fills up fast for the Classic. Cabins, the Lodge, and vacation rentals are all within riding distance of the start.

🔧

Mechanical Support

On-course mechanical support and SAG vehicles on all routes. Aid stations stocked with water, electrolytes, and nutrition every 15 miles.

🌡️

Altitude Tips

Arrive a day early to acclimate. Hydrate aggressively. Expect temperatures 15-20 degrees cooler than the desert below. Sunscreen is essential at altitude.

🍽️

Post-Ride Dining

Refuel at Cloudcroft's restaurants. Mad Jack's BBQ, Big Daddy' Diner, and Cloudcroft Brewery are rider favorites for recovery meals.
