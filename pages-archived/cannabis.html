Recreational Cannabis in the Sacramento Mountains

New Mexico legalized recreational cannabis for adults 21 and older in 2021, with retail sales beginning on April 1, 2022. Cloudcroft welcomed its first dispensaries that same spring, offering visitors and locals alike access to legal cannabis products high in the Sacramento Mountains.

Today, Cloudcroft is home to three licensed dispensaries, each offering a curated selection of flower, edibles, concentrates, and more. Whether you're a first-time visitor or an experienced consumer, the friendly staff at our local shops are happy to help you find the right products for your mountain getaway.

Please consume responsibly and follow all New Mexico cannabis laws during your visit.

Know the Law

Where You Can & Cannot Consume

Understanding New Mexico's cannabis consumption laws will help you stay compliant and enjoy your visit responsibly.

✓ Where You CAN Consume

  • Private residences where you have permission
  • Vacation rentals that explicitly allow cannabis use
  • Private property with the owner's consent
  • Licensed cannabis consumption lounges (where available)

✗ Where You CANNOT Consume

  • Any public place (sidewalks, parks, streets)
  • In any motor vehicle (driver or passenger)
  • Federal lands (Lincoln National Forest, etc.)
  • Hotels/lodging unless explicitly permitted
  • Near schools or childcare facilities
  • Any property where landlord prohibits use

✗ What's NOT Allowed

  • Driving under the influence of cannabis
  • Providing cannabis to anyone under 21
  • Taking cannabis across state lines
  • Possessing more than 2 oz in public
  • Consuming on federal property
  • Reselling cannabis products

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Federal Lands: The Lincoln National Forest and other federal lands around Cloudcroft still prohibit cannabis, even though it's legal in New Mexico. This includes all hiking trails and camping areas in the national forest.
  • Texas Visitors: Cannabis remains illegal in Texas. Do not transport any cannabis products across state lines—this is a federal offense.
  • Lodging: Always check with your hotel, cabin rental, or vacation property about their cannabis policy before consuming on the premises.
  • Altitude: At 9,000 feet elevation, cannabis effects may feel stronger. Start low and go slow, especially if you're new to cannabis or visiting from lower elevations.
  • Valid ID: You must be 21+ and present valid government-issued photo ID to purchase cannabis products.
Visitor Tips

First Time at a Dispensary?

Many visitors to Cloudcroft are experiencing legal cannabis for the first time. Here's what to expect.

🪪

Bring Valid ID

You'll need a government-issued photo ID proving you're 21 or older. Driver's license, state ID, or passport all work.

💵

Cash is King

Many dispensaries are cash-only due to federal banking restrictions. ATMs are usually available on-site.

💬

Ask Questions

Budtenders are knowledgeable and happy to help. Don't be shy about asking for recommendations based on your experience level.

🌱

Start Low, Go Slow

Especially with edibles, start with a low dose (5-10mg) and wait at least 2 hours before consuming more.

🏔️

Mind the Altitude

At 9,000 feet, effects can be intensified. Stay hydrated and take it easy, especially on your first day.

🚗

Plan Ahead

Never drive under the influence. Plan your activities and have a safe place to enjoy your products responsibly.
